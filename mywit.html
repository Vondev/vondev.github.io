MyWit

Over the summer of 2018, I interned at Assurant, an insurance company based in Atlanta. MyWit.com is an online e-commerce store run by Assurant that sells used phones and tablets. In this project, I designed a new resource center for the site by conducting user research while balancing different business needs. This includes user research, crafting wireframes, reworking the information architecture of the site, and reimagining its visual design.

Problem

A company's side projects often do not receive the same time and resources devoted to its main product line. Such is arguably the fate of MyWit.com — an e-commerce site run by Assurant that sells used phones and tablets. Sales have been underperforming since the inception of the site. In fact, given the large inflow of used devices sourced from Assurant's mobile suppliers, our data team estimates that our current revenue is only a fifth of our potential revenue. There is also a general consensus among our design team that MyWit performs poorly on all 3 measures of branding, usability, and aesthetics.

Old Homepage for MyWit.com

The Plan

As part of new efforts to improve the design of the MyWit.com, the product owner had plans to develop a resource center for the site. This resource center would house things like help articles, buying tips, tech news, and other traffic generating resources created by the marketing department. It would also serve as a secondary point of contact for the site's partner's and suppliers, housing the MyWit logo, images, and other media. I was put in charge of leading this redesign effort, which ultimately spanned the last 4 weeks of my internship at Assurant.

Baby Steps

My first instinct was to design new brand standards and an improved design system for MyWit. However, after a few in-depth discussions with the product owner, we agreed that it would be better to take baby steps and integrate the new resource center into the site with design improvements that keep to the current brand standards. The resource center would then serve as a launchpad for an future overhaul of the rest of the site. Hence, my redesign would be subject to the limitations below.
